\begin{abstract}
Many computational physiscs problems involve the many-body Schr√∂dinger equation (SE) and modelling of quantum dots is one of them. The problem with the many-body SE is that it is very hard or impossible to solve it analytically. Therefore, numerical solution are necessary to gain insight into the behaviour of many-body systems of particles. In this particular project I am working with quantum Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) methods to solve the many-body SE for electrons in a harmonic oscillator trap, which here represents the quantum dot. I am looking at full-shell systems of two, six and twelve electrons in harmonic oscillator traps of different strengths, i.e. trap frequencies, and investigating the different systems by comparing expectation energies and one-body densities. Additionally, I look at how these properties change by introducing interaction between the particles. 
\end{abstract}
